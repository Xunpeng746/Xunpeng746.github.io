Overview
Stochastic Projected Approximate Newton (SPAN) is a novel approximate and fast Newton method for minimizing general convex/strongly convex objective functions. Different from previous second-order methods, SPAN can both prevent from calculating exact Hessian matrices and control the error between approximate and exact Hessian. This is achieved by (a) the introduction of randomized low rank approximation and (b) the scant equation of objective functions. SPAN not only provide a improved theoretical complexity but also the SOTA emperical performances in some tasks.
